Car and Art SPARK!
To support Gleaners Food Bank!
The annual SPARK! Fishers Car and Art Show brings enthusiasts and artists together to explore and exhibit the intersection of car culture and arts culture while benefiting a local charity. Attendees stroll among vehicles while artists create and exhibit their work. Proceeds generated through sponsorships and car registrations benefit a community committee chosen not-for-profit organization each year.

Register YOUR Car!

Registration Info

Space limits the number of registered vehicles to roughly 80. Registration will be accepted on a first-come, first-served basis through June 20. Individuals registering their vehicles can choose from a “Premier” parking spot, with higher visibility and foot traffic, or a “Standard” parking spot within the event footprint. See event map for these locations. All registered attendees will receive a t-shirt and poster. All event profits will go to (Organization TBD) Gleaners Food Bank.

Artists and Artwork
Art Information

The Spark!Fishers Car & Art Show will take place on June 24th from 6-9 pm at the Central Green in the Nickel Plate District, a designated Arts & Culture District certified by the Indiana Arts Commission. We expect 30 artists and artisans will display their art, much of it with a racing or car theme. Our featured artist is Gary Dausch www.gdausch.com. Gary will bring his mobile 28-foot gallery to the event. He will offer original art; limited edition prints and apparel. His gallery will be in front of city hall. Besides Gary we will have a variety of artists and artisans to make this a family-friendly event. Two of our favorite artists, Greg Clack and Walt Thacker will actually be painting at the show. Make sure to stop by and check them out. All of the artist/artisan tents will be on the Central Green and the east and west flanks of city hall.



Guidance for attending artists

Artists registered for the event need to plan to arrive between 4:00 and 5:30- 1.5 hours prior to closure of the roads within the event footprint. All artists are expected to be masked. Sanitizer will be provided to each artist. Any artist can refuse service to any attendee that is not masked to ensure their personal safety
